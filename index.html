① Backflow Preventer

Screws directly onto hose bib. Prevents water in the irrigation line from ever siphoning back into your home supply. Required by most codes.

② Smart Timer (2-zone)

Controls Zone 1 (tomatoes) and Zone 2 (everything else) on independent schedules. Choose one with weather-skip (Rachio 3, Orbit B-hyve, Rain Bird). Connects between BFP and pressure regulator.

③ Pressure Regulator

Steps your bib pressure down to 25–30 PSI. This is not optional — without it emitters will blow out or mist instead of drip.

④ Y-Filter (150 mesh)

Catches sediment that would clog emitters over time. Installed after the regulator. Clean out the screen at the start of each season.

Trunk & Laterals

3/4" PVC runs east-west along the house. Three 1/2" PVC laterals drop south, one per column. Each lateral ends with an end cap. A barbed tee fitting on the lateral is where water branches off into each bed via flexible poly tubing.

What You're Looking At

This is a side view of one bed. The PVC lateral runs along the outside of the bed. Water branches off the lateral at a barbed tee fitting, travels through flexible poly tubing into the bed, and drips from emitters directly at soil level under the mulch.

Step by Step

1
Punch a hole in the lateral

Use a dedicated hole punch tool. Position it at the height you want the tubing to enter the bed — typically at mid-wall height.

2
Press in the barbed tee

The barb grabs the pipe wall. One side stays in the hole, one side faces toward the bed. No glue, no clamps — just press firmly.

3
Run poly tubing into the bed

Cut a length of 1/2" poly tubing. Leave a 6" service loop at the entry point — that slack lets you adjust later without stressing the barb. Route the tubing along the inside of the bed wall.

4
Install emitters

Punch holes in the poly tubing, press emitters in. 4 per bed, spaced evenly. 1 GPH for most crops, 2 GPH for tomatoes.

5
Pin tubing with stakes

Plastic tubing stakes every 8–12 inches keep the line flat against the soil. Without stakes it lifts and wanders over time.

6
Cap the end

Fold the end of the poly tubing and secure with an end cap, or use a goof plug on the final hole. If you mis-punch a hole anywhere, fill it with a goof plug.

Tips

Buy twice as many goof plugs as you think you need — mis-punches happen to everyone. Warm poly tubing in the sun before routing it; cold tubing is stiff and fights you. The tubing can sit on top of the soil under the mulch — no need to bury it.
