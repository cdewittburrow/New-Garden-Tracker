① Backflow Preventer

Screws directly onto hose bib. Prevents siphoning back into your home supply. Required by most codes.

② Smart Timer (2-zone)

Controls Zone 1 (tomatoes) and Zone 2 on independent schedules. Choose one with weather-skip — Rachio 3, Orbit B-hyve, Rain Bird.

③ Pressure Regulator

Steps bib pressure down to 25–30 PSI. Not optional — without it emitters blow out or mist instead of drip.

④ Y-Filter (150 mesh)

Catches sediment that would clog emitters. Clean the screen at the start of each season.

Trunk & Laterals

3/4" PVC trunk runs east-west. Three 1/2" laterals drop south. A barbed tee on each lateral feeds each bed via poly tubing.

Drip system

What You're Looking At

A side view of one bed. The PVC lateral runs outside. Water branches off via a barbed tee, travels through poly tubing into the bed, and drips from emitters at soil level under the mulch.

Step by Step

1
Punch a hole in the lateral

Use a hole punch tool at mid-wall height.

2
Press in the barbed tee

The barb grabs the pipe wall. No glue needed.

3
Run poly tubing into the bed

Leave a 6" service loop at entry.

4
Install emitters

4 per bed. 1 GPH most crops, 2 GPH tomatoes.

5
Pin tubing with stakes

Every 8–12 inches.

6
Cap the end

Fold + end cap or goof plug.

Tips

Buy twice as many goof plugs as you think you need. Warm poly tubing in the sun before routing. No burying needed.

Cattle Panel Arch

A 16-ft cattle panel bent over the 2×4 bed forms a self-supporting tunnel arch ~30" tall at the peak. Rebar stakes driven into the bed ends anchor both sides.

Setup Notes

1
Bend the panel

Stand the 16-ft panel on edge and walk both ends toward each other.

2
Drive rebar

2-ft rebar stakes inside the bed walls, one per side. Thread the panel ends over them.

3
Frost cloth

Drape over the arch and pin at the base with soil staples or bricks. Remove once overnight temps stay above 40°F.

4
Train the vines

Peas climb naturally. Gently weave early tendrils into the mesh so they grab on.

Why This Works

The arch doubles vertical growing space, keeps vines off soil (less disease), and the frost cloth turns it into a mini cold frame in early spring — extending the season by 4–6 weeks.
